Erica Nicole Hall - Engineering Portfolio

Recent graduate of the University of Alabama at Birmingham (UAB), where I studied biomedical and mechanical engineering as well as chemistry, resulting in a Bachelor's of Science in Biomedical Engineering awarded in May 2025. I also earned Magna cum Laude and distinguished honors through the Science and Technology Honors Program. During my undergraduate studies, I worked in the Tissue and Organ Chip Bioengineering Laboratory investigating the role of pneumonia infection in major adverse cardiac events in patients months to years after hospitalization.

This website showcases my projects both in and outside of my coursework and what I have learned from these experiences. Please see the "get in touch" section for the most recent copy of my resume and my contact information.

Course Projects

Projects developed as  part of course requirements for both biomedical and mechanical engineering. They largely center around innovation, computer aided design, computational fluid dynamics, and programming. Softwares and languages used include SolidWorks, ANSYS, and MATLAB.

Research Projects

My research involves the investigation of the role of pneumonia infection in contributing to major adverse cardiac events later on. This work also aims to characterize the physiological differences in 2D and 3D models, validating the use of 3D tissue chips.
